No. 3: The Craig Countercurrent Distribution Train, December 2010.
The Oesper Apparatus Collection has recently acquired a rare, surviving example of a 30-cell Craig Countercurrent Distribution Train. This apparatus, described in 1949 by Dr. Lyman C. Craig of the Rockefeller Institute for Medical Research, formed a key advance in liquid-liquid extraction techniques. Dr. Robley Williams, a former student of Craig, drove the unit across country from his home in Colorado to donate it to the Oesper Collections.
No. 2: Oesper's Salt, November 2010.
This issue of Notes from the Oesper Collections describes the introduction in 1947 by Ralph Edward Oesper of a new oxidimetric standard now known as "Oesper's Salt."
No. 1: Found Tucked in a Book, October 2010.
The first issue of Notes from the Oesper Collections describes the discovery, tucked into the pages of rare books, of previously unknown photographs of notable American chemists.
